Provenance

Quarried from a clay seam we refuse to name.

Somewhere along an undisclosed riverbank, a vein of clay has been resting for eleven thousand years, waiting for absolutely nothing in particular — until now.

We extract it by hand, in small batches, on days our master mason deems the light "correct." No GPS coordinates exist. No satellite has seen it. Ask us anyway; we still won't tell you.

— Atelier Notes, Vol. I
The Firing

Thirty-six hours at 1,200°C. Zero shortcuts.

Every Brique is fired slowly, by hand-fed kiln, until the clay forgets it was ever soft. We could do it faster. We choose not to.
